02 · Mechanism

A controlled path to ground.
Not a hope and a prayer.

OEM systems rely on an air-gap spark. SLPS replaces that gap with continuous brush contact — every microamp finds the path of least resistance, on purpose.

IEC 61400-24
  1. 01

    Strike

    A leader contacts the blade tip receptor. Up to 200 kA at the entry point.

  2. 02

    Capture

    Down-conductor carries current through the blade root into the hub.

  3. 03

    Bridge

    SLPS brushes replace the OEM air-gap with continuous low-impedance contact across the rotating joint.

  4. 04

    Discharge

    Energy is shunted down the tower to the foundation grid — silently, safely, every time.

05 · Stakes

One bad storm doesn’t stay one bad storm.

A single uncontrolled discharge cascades into bearing damage, sensor faults, weeks of crane time and lost MWh. SLPS breaks the chain at step one.

  1. 01 · Strike

    Uncontrolled discharge

    Air-gap spark fires randomly across rotating joints. Hundreds of micro-events per season.

  2. 02 · Hours later

    Nuisance trip

    Static interference triggers 2106 / 2118 alarms. Turbine curtails. SCADA queues a service ticket.

  3. 03 · Days later

    Component damage

    Bearing currents pit raceways. Pitch motors degrade. Generator slip rings burn.

  4. SLPS · Day zero

    Chain broken

    Continuous brush contact dissipates the discharge before it can cascade. Turbine stays online.
